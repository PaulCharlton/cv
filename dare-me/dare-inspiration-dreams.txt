Dare Me to Do It Again — Inspiration Dreams Edition

I wasn’t born with the keys.
I wasn’t handed the manual.

I was raised on signal.
Myth.
Mistakes.

On movies that got the tech wrong
but got the longing right.

Ally Sheedy made curiosity feel holy.
Angelina Jolie made the keyboard feel like a blade.
Most sidekicks got lost in the fade.
But Sheedy leaned in. Jolie led with the edge.

TRON showed us light had geometry.
Lawnmower Man asked if the mind could upgrade.
The Matrix told us reality was a patch.
Minority Report taught us to dance with the interface.
Eagle Eye whispered that the system might already be alive.

We didn’t watch.
We absorbed.
And then we started building.

And the books —
they were our scripture.
    •  Neuromancer: where silence had syntax.
    •  Snow Crash: where language became payload.
    •  Cryptonomicon: where math had lineage and teeth.
    •  True Names: where privacy was the last spell.
    •  Shockwave Rider: where identity ran on a thread.
    •  Mirrorshades: where the culture forked.

But beyond it all, behind it all —
Lanier.

Jaron didn’t want to hack the system.
He wanted to merge with it.
To shape it like a cuttlefish.
To listen to latency like language.
To feel feedback loops like heartbeat.

He reminded us:
You don’t need a GUI if your soul is the interface.

That’s where I grew.
That’s where I turned dream into protocol.
That’s where I learned that inspiration wasn’t escape —
it was compilation.

⸻

So if you ask me today,
“Could you build again?”
“Could you go back in?”
“Could you light it all up?”

Here’s your answer:

Dare me.
I’m not just dreaming of the future.
I’m writing it from memory.
