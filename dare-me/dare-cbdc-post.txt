Dare Me To Do It Again: CBDC 2018

I was asked to build a nation’s digital currency. I said no.

In 2018, I was invited by a senior figure in the government of a BRICS nation to architect their Central Bank Digital Currency.

This wasn’t theoretical. I reviewed requirements. I met with key members of the Prime Minister’s administration. I was ready to deliver a system that would define their digital M0 — the foundation of sovereign money in the 21st century.

But I had two conditions:
	1.	Absolute anonymity for users.
Not pseudonymity. Not “private unless flagged.”
True, cryptographically enforced anonymity.
Because without private money, there is no economic freedom.
	2.	Full control of national infrastructure.
	•	Retain digital sovereignty.
	•	Don’t outsource the DNS root.
	•	Don’t outsource the national CA.
	•	And above all: adopt post-quantum encryption and zero-trust security.

I wasn’t going to build a digital monetary base on a compromised substrate. When someone eventually stole billions, I wasn’t going to be the architect blamed for the breach.

They declined.

The PM’s cybersecurity chief asked me to “show him” what I meant.

I asked for permission.

Two minutes later, I had root on one of the nation’s primary DNS servers.

We both understood what that meant.

I thanked him for the tea — and walked away from the deal.

Because I don’t build systems I can’t stand behind.
